What is a data breach?

An incident in which sensitive, protected, or confidential data has potentially been viewed, stolen, or used by an individual unauthorized to do so.

What type of data can be breached?

Data breaches may involve payment card information (PCI), personal health information (PHI), personally identifiable information (PII), trade secrets, or intellectual property.

How costly is a data breach?

The average cost per record lost in a data breach is $148 and the average total cost of a breach is $3.86 million.

What are some common type of data breaches?

Most organizations expose data through hacking or negligence but data breaches are also caused by ransomware, malware, phishing, and denial-of-service

How often are data breaches occuring?

In 2019, the number of data breaches in the United States amounted to 1,473 with over 164.68 million sensitive records exposed.

Who is the victim of data breaches?

Although data breaches have a tendency to target larger companies, millions of customers can be placed in the line of fire by association.
